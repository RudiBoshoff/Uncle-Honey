How Honey is Made

Nectar Collection

Honey bees collect pollen and nectar from flowers and plants and store it in their stomachs. While this nectar is inside the bee's stomach, the nectar mixes with proteins and enzymes that convert the nectar into honey.

Filling Comb with Honey

Once back at the hive, the bees regurgitate the honey into a beeswax comb, which is a collection of hexagonal cells made from wax produced by the bees. The bees repeat the process until the combs are full.

Long Term Storage

To prepare for long-term storage, the bees fan their wings to evaporate moisture from the honey in order to thicken it. Once the honey is at the desired consistency, the bees cap the honeycomb with wax and move on to the next empty comb, starting all over again.

Honey Extraction

Once the combs are full, the honey is extracted from the hive in a bee friendly manner by using a chemical-free smoker to gently smoke the hives. The smoke calms the bees so that they are less deffensive which prevents the bees from getting hurt during the process.

Production

Once the honey is extracted from the hive, it is then packaged raw as well as processed into other products. The result: a stunning variety of honey products for you to spoil yourself or a loved one.

Certified Sustainable Honey

​​What is sustainable honey? If the bees do not put it there, neither do we. We do not use any antibiotics, pesticides, fungicides, or any other "-cides" in our hives. We only sustainably harvest surplus as they create honey to sustain their population. We never take honey and feed back sugar water instead. We at Uncle Honey adhere stricly to the guides imposed by the European Union Regulation for Organic Beekeeping.
